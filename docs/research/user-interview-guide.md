# EV Scooter Calculator - User Interview Guide

## Interview Overview

**Purpose:** Understand user behaviors, pain points, and feature preferences for the EV Scooter Calculator redesign.

**Duration:** 45 minutes
**Participants:** 20 users (5 per persona segment)
**Incentive:** $150 per participant

## Participant Segments

1. **Tech-Savvy Enthusiast** - DIY modifiers, wants granular control
2. **Casual Rider** - Wants simple upgrade recommendations, minimal complexity
3. **Fleet Manager** - Needs efficiency comparisons, bulk analysis tools
4. **First-Time Buyer** - Needs guidance, education, confidence building

## Interview Questions

### Introduction (5 minutes)

1. How did you first hear about EV scooter calculators?
2. What motivated you to try the EV Scooter Calculator?
3. How often do you use scooters personally or professionally?

### Current Usage & Workflows (10 minutes)

4. Walk me through the last time you used the calculator - what did you do?
5. Which features do you use most frequently?
6. Which features do you never use or avoid?
7. How do you typically enter your scooter's specifications (presets, manual entry)?
8. What do you do with the results/analysis the calculator provides?

### Pain Points & Challenges (10 minutes)

9. What was the most confusing part of using the calculator?
10. Were there any terms or metrics you didn't understand?
11. At what point did you consider stopping or giving up?
12. What frustrated you most during the experience?

### Feature Requests & Improvements (10 minutes)

13. What features would make this tool more useful for you?
14. If you could change one thing about the interface, what would it be?
15. How do you prefer to access the calculator (mobile, tablet, desktop)?

### Conclusion (10 minutes)

16. What's your overall satisfaction with the calculator on a scale of 1-10?
17. Would you recommend this tool to others? Why or why not?
18. Is there anything else we should know about your experience?

## Technical Terms to Probe

During the interview, observe understanding of these terms and note confusion:

- C-rate (battery discharge rate)
- Voltage sag
- Controller limits
- Drivetrain efficiency
- State of Health (SoH)
- Regeneration braking
- Wheel size impact
- Riding style consumption (Wh/km)

## Data Collection Notes

For each participant, record:
- Persona segment
- Device used (mobile, tablet, desktop)
- Browser
- Session duration
- Time on task (if applicable)
- Confusion points identified
- Feature requests
- Quote highlights

## Post-Interview Follow-Up

- Send thank-you email with incentive details
- Ask for follow-up availability (if needed for clarifications)
- Include link to share additional feedback privately
